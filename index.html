Deterministic-to-Stochastic Diverse Latent Feature Mapping for Human Motion Synthesis

Paper arXiv Video Code

Abstract

Human motion synthesis aims to produce plausible human motion sequences, which has raised widespread attention in computer animation. Recent score-based generative models (SGMs) have demonstrated impressive results on this task. However, their training process involves complex curvature trajectories, leading to unstable training process and slow sampling speeds. To alleviate this, we propose a Deterministic-to-Stochastic Diverse Latent Feature Mapping (DSDFM) method for human motion synthesis. DSDFM consists of two stages. The first stage aims to learn the latent space distribution of human motions through the human motion reconstruction process. The second stage aims to build connections between the Gaussian distribution and the latent space distribution of human motions, thereby enhancing the diversity and accuracy of the generated human motions. This stage is achieved by the designed deterministic feature mapping procedure with DerODE and stochastic diverse output generation procedure with DivSDE. DSDFM can enhance the diversity and accuracy of generated human motions, which is easy to train compared to previous SGMs-based methods. Through qualitative and quantitative experiments, DSDFM achieves state-of-the-art results surpassing the latest methods, validating its superiority in unconditional human motion synthesis.

Fig. 1. Examples of the unconditional human motion synthesis. Our method aims to generate highly diverse human motion sequences from unstructured and unlabeled motion data.

Mehtods

The proposed DSDFM can synthesize diverse and natural human motion sequences through the above-mentioned two stages, i.e., human motion reconstruction and diverse motion generation. In the human motion reconstruction stage, we first adopt the human motion reconstruction network to learn a well-structured latent space of human motions through VQVAE network, where the encoder-decoder network is implemented by the Transformer and GRU models. In the diverse motion generation stage, we tend to build the connections between the Gaussian and latent space of human motions, thereby enhancing the diversity while guaranteeing the accuracy of the generated human motions through the designed deterministic feature mapping procedure with DerODE and stochastic diverse output generation procedure with DivSDE. Specifically, DerODE can first provide a deterministic output results efficiently. Meanwhile DivSDE can obtain more diverse human motions with the aid of stochastic differential equations without re-introducing other training processes.
